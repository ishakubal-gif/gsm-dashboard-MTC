💡 Understanding RSSI Metrics

What is it? RSSI stands for Received Signal Strength Indicator. It mathematically measures exact wireless signal transmission robustness hitting your hardware node at this precise location.

Why is it negative? In telecom, receiving power is mapped in decibel-milliwatts (dBm). Because nodes receive tiny fractional subsets of a single milliwatt from distant repeater towers, the value is heavily negative. The closer you are to 0, the stronger the signal!

Scientific Reference Scale

Strong (-55) Mid (-75) Weak (-95) Dead (-115)
